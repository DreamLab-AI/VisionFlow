## What was written

`docs/DDD-judgment-broker-context.md` was extended in two places, per ADR-005
§Decision 5:

1. **Invariant 5 extended** from *"Decision immutability"* to *"Decision
   immutability and supersession authority"*: supersession is authority-gated — only
   the original human signer or a higher governance role may supersede a published
   `DecisionOutcome`, and only by a new signed kind-31403 referencing the superseded
   event with a stated reason. Immutability is preserved (no event is mutated).

2. **New §7a Supersession Authority (F6)** — the specification the forum implements
   against:
   - **§7a.1 Who may supersede** — the original signer, or a human of a governance
     role above the original at supersession time; an equal/lower role MUST NOT
     supersede; an agent MUST NOT (Invariant 4). `RelayGovernanceGate` rejects an
     unauthorised superseding kind-31403.
   - **§7a.2 How** — a new signed kind-31403 that (1) `e`-tags the superseded event,
     (2) carries the superseding signer's `DecisionOutcome`, (3) states a reason.
     **Revoke** = supersession by a `Reject` referencing a prior `Approve`.
     **Appeal** = a fresh kind-31402 citing the prior decision (reopens; does not
     itself overturn).
   - **§7a.3 Lifecycle — Superseded / Reopened** — two new states over
     `CaseState {Open, PendingReview, Resolved, Rejected}`, with the transition
     diagram; the effective decision is the most recent authorised kind-31403 in the
     reference chain; superseded events remain visible as history.
   - **§7a.4 Ownership/integration** — the forum owns `DecisionOutcome` and the state
     machine and implements §7a; a cross-context extension through the owner, not a
     parallel model.

The five new terms (Supersession, Revoke, Appeal, Superseded, Reopened) were added to
the §8 Ubiquitous Language table for internal coherence.

## Falsification exposure

Falsified if the spec permits an equal/lower-role human or an agent to supersede, if
supersession is modelled as a mutation of the original event rather than a new
referencing kind-31403, if the lifecycle omits Superseded/Reopened, or if F6 is
claimed `integrated` before the forum cites §7a and demonstrates an authorised vs
unauthorised supersede end to end.
