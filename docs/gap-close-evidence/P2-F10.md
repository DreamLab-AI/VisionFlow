## What was written

`docs/protocol/forum-social-dynamics.md` — a new `docs/protocol/` position page
alongside `identity-spine.md`, stating the canon position on multi-agent social
influence in forum threads. It is grounded in the three sources the PRD names:

1. **The register finding.** Chapter 14b forum register: *"Silent on multi-agent
   social influence in threads … theory-to-canon … Minor"*, and *"the canon is
   silent on the emergent social influence of several agents co-present in one
   thread, a measurable effect given the live six-agent DreamLab roster"*.
2. **The book's Chapter 14b / Chapter 5 framing.** The "ant death spiral — agents
   responding to agents in loops" as a system-level vigilance failure, and the
   book's escalation posture ("concentrates scarce human attention on cases that
   are genuinely novel, ambiguous, or high-stakes").
3. **The disclosure MUST (COM-13).** The page builds on `identity-spine.md`
   §"Agent Disclosure Norm (COM-13)" and does not restate it: COM-13 makes each
   agent individually legible; F10 governs the aggregate group effect.

It carries RFC 2119 normative positions on the three axes the task requires:

- **Herding / quorum (§3.1):** agent consensus MUST NOT count toward a human
  quorum or decision threshold; human and agent contributions reported separately;
  agent contributions collapsible so volume is not read as social proof.
- **Badge-visible agent density per thread (§3.2):** a conformant surface MUST show
  the per-thread distinct-agent count and, on demand, identities + authorising
  principals resolved from the COM-13 registry; shared-principal agents disclosed.
- **Escalation posture on amplification (§3.3):** agent-to-agent amplification MUST
  NOT increase decision weight; MUST be attributable (not anonymous social proof);
  MUST escalate to a human above a density/endorsement threshold rather than
  auto-resolve.

A §4 table states exactly what a conformant forum surface must show a human; §6
names the two integration consumers.
