## Disruptive Technologies-Specific Guidelines

### Technical Depth
- Explain the fundamental innovation or breakthrough
- Describe what makes it disruptive (not just sustaining improvement)
- Discuss adoption dynamics and market transformation
- Include both technical and market/business perspectives
- Address societal and ethical implications

### Linking Strategy
- Link to innovation frameworks ([[Disruptive Innovation]], [[Technology Adoption Lifecycle]], [[Gartner Hype Cycle]])
- Link to enabling technologies ([[AI]], [[Blockchain]], [[Quantum Computing]], etc.)
- Link to disrupted industries ([[Healthcare]], [[Finance]], [[Transportation]], etc.)
- Link to complementary innovations (cross-domain connections)
- Link to policy and governance concepts ([[Regulation]], [[Ethics]], [[Responsible Innovation]])

### UK Disruptive Technology Context
- Emphasise UK research institutions and innovation hubs (Cambridge, Oxford, London, Edinburgh)
- Highlight UK companies and startups in this technology space
- Note UK Government initiatives (Industrial Strategy, Catapults, Innovate UK, sector deals)
- Include UK regulatory approaches and policy frameworks
- Reference regional clusters (Cambridge tech, Oxford biotech, London fintech, Manchester advanced manufacturing)

### Common Disruptive Technologies Sections
- Disruption Characteristics (for all disruptive innovations—explain type and mechanism of disruption)
- Market and Industry Impact (market sizing, industries disrupted, economic impact)
- Adoption Dynamics (adoption curve, hype cycle, barriers, accelerators)
- Societal and Ethical Implications (broader impacts beyond economics and technology)
- Enablers and Dependencies (prerequisite or complementary technologies, infrastructure, ecosystem)
