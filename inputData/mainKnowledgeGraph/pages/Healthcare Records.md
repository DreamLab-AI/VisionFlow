## Healthcare Records

Healthcare Records refers to blockchain-based electronic health record systems employing immutable distributed ledgers, smart contracts for consent management, and cryptographic security measures to enable secure patient data sharing, interoperability across healthcare providers, and patient-controlled access whilst maintaining hipaa and gdpr compliance through hybrid on-chain/off-chain architectures.

- Industry adoption and implementations
  - Blockchain-based electronic health record (EHR) systems are increasingly deployed by hospitals, insurers, and research institutions to enable secure, auditable, and interoperable data exchange
  - Notable platforms include IBM Lifescience, ConsenSys Health, and Embleema, with growing interest from NHS Digital and regional health authorities
  - In North England, Manchester University NHS Foundation Trust and Leeds Teaching Hospitals NHS Trust have piloted blockchain solutions for consent management and clinical data sharing, with Newcastle and Sheffield exploring similar initiatives
  - These implementations often leverage hybrid on-chain/off-chain architectures to balance performance, privacy, and regulatory compliance
- Technical capabilities and limitations
  - Immutable distributed ledgers ensure data integrity and auditability, reducing the risk of tampering and unauthorised access
  - Smart contracts automate consent workflows and access controls, streamlining patient authorisation and regulatory compliance
  - Cryptographic security measures, such as zero-knowledge proofs and homomorphic encryption, enhance privacy while enabling selective data sharing
  - Limitations include scalability challenges, integration complexity with legacy systems, and the need for robust governance frameworks
- Standards and frameworks
  - Compliance with UK and EU regulations, such as the Data Protection Act 2018 and GDPR, is achieved through careful design of access controls and audit trails
  - Industry standards, including HL7 FHIR and ISO/TS 18308, are increasingly integrated with blockchain architectures to ensure interoperability and data portability

- British contributions and implementations
  - The UK has been at the forefront of blockchain innovation in healthcare, with NHS Digital and the National Institute for Health Research (NIHR) supporting pilot projects and research initiatives
  - The NHS has explored blockchain for secure data sharing, consent management, and supply chain integrity, particularly in response to the challenges of interoperability and data security
