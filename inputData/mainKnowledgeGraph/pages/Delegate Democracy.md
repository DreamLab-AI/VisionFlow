## Delegate Democracy

Delegate Democracy refers to a component of the blockchain ecosystem.

- Delegate democracy is increasingly adopted in blockchain governance, especially within DAOs managing significant assets and protocol decisions.
  - Notable platforms include Uniswap DAO, MakerDAO, Aave DAO, and Cardano’s governance system, which incorporates liquid democracy principles.
  - Innovations such as "RevoteChain" introduce revocable delegation, allowing voters to reclaim or reassign their votes dynamically, enhancing responsiveness and trust.
- Technical capabilities:
  - Blockchain ensures immutability and transparency of votes and delegations.
  - Smart contracts automate delegation logic but cannot fully prevent governance manipulation or coordination failures.
  - Systems support metadelegation, where votes can be delegated through multiple intermediaries, increasing flexibility but also complexity.
- Limitations include potential concentration of voting power, Sybil attacks, and challenges in voter engagement and education.
- Standards and frameworks are emerging, with proposals like Cardano Improvement Proposal CIP-1694 formalising delegation mechanisms and governance protocols.
