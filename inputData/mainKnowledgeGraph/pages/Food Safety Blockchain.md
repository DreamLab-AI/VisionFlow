## Food Safety Blockchain

Food Safety Blockchain refers to blockchain applications addressing critical food safety challenges through immutable farm-to-fork audit trails, enabling rapid contamination source identification (2.2 seconds vs 6+ days traditional trace-back) and surgical recalls that reduce food waste whilst protecting consumers. major implementations including ibm food trust (500+ participants including walmart, carrefour, dole, nestlé) and vechain's walmart china deployment demonstrate transformative impact on the £55 billion annual foodborne illness problem affecting 48 million americans, with 50-70% reductions in trace-back time and 30% sales increases for blockchain-tracked products through enhanced consumer trust.

- Industry adoption and implementations
	- Blockchain platforms are widely adopted by major retailers and food producers globally to ensure product authenticity and streamline recalls
	- Notable organisations and platforms
		- IBM Food Trust remains a leading platform, with participation from global brands such as Walmart, Carrefour, Dole, and Nestlé
		- VeChain has expanded its footprint, supporting traceability initiatives in multiple markets, including China and Europe
	- UK and North England examples where relevant
		- UK retailers such as Tesco and Sainsbury’s have piloted blockchain for high-risk produce, with trials in Manchester and Leeds focusing on leafy greens and dairy
		- Newcastle-based agritech startups are collaborating with local farmers to pilot blockchain-enabled traceability for regional supply chains
- Technical capabilities and limitations
	- Blockchain enables real-time, tamper-proof recording of food provenance, storage conditions, and transaction history
	- Limitations include scalability challenges, interoperability between platforms, and the need for robust data governance
- Standards and frameworks
	- Industry standards such as GS1’s blockchain guidelines and ISO/IEC 20547 for blockchain and distributed ledger technologies are shaping best practices
	- Regulatory frameworks in the UK and EU are evolving to support blockchain adoption while ensuring data privacy and consumer protection
