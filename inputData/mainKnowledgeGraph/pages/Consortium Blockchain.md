## Consortium Blockchain

Consortium Blockchain refers to a component of the blockchain ecosystem.

- Industry adoption and implementations
	- Consortium blockchains are widely adopted in sectors such as finance, supply chain, healthcare, and energy, where multiple stakeholders need to collaborate securely
	- Notable organisations and platforms
		- Kaleido provides a comprehensive suite of tools for consortium blockchain development, supporting a range of industries
		- IBM and AWS offer robust platforms for consortium blockchain networks, facilitating collaboration and data sharing
	- UK and North England examples where relevant
		- In Manchester, consortium blockchains are being explored for supply chain management in the textile industry, enhancing transparency and efficiency
		- Leeds has seen initiatives in healthcare, where consortium blockchains are used to securely share patient data among different providers
		- Newcastle and Sheffield are home to innovation hubs that are piloting consortium blockchains for energy trading and smart city applications
- Technical capabilities and limitations
	- Consortium blockchains offer faster transaction speeds due to a smaller number of nodes involved in the validation process
	- Data privacy is enhanced as only a select group of users have access to the network, reducing the risk of data breaches
	- However, the limited transparency and potential for centralisation can be drawbacks, especially in scenarios where full decentralisation is desired
- Standards and frameworks
	- The development of consortium blockchains is guided by industry standards and frameworks, such as those from the International Organization for Standardization (ISO) and the European Blockchain Partnership (EBP)
	- These standards help ensure interoperability, security, and compliance across different consortium blockchain networks
