## Self Sovereign Identity

Self Sovereign Identity refers to a component of the blockchain ecosystem.

- SSI adoption has accelerated across industries seeking secure, privacy-preserving digital identity solutions, especially within Web3 ecosystems.
  - Leading platforms provide standards-based verifiable credentials, decentralised ID wallets, and biometric-bound credentials to enhance security and user control.
  - Notable organisations include Truvera, Oracle Blockchain, and various open-source initiatives advancing SSI protocols and privacy-enhanced credentials[1][4].
- Technical capabilities:
  - SSI systems leverage blockchain and peer-to-peer networks to establish a trust triangle among issuers, holders, and verifiers.
  - Privacy is enhanced by selective disclosure, allowing users to share only necessary identity attributes.
  - Limitations include scalability challenges, interoperability hurdles, and the need for widespread adoption of common standards.
- Standards and frameworks:
  - The ecosystem is guided by standards such as W3C’s Verifiable Credentials and Decentralised Identifiers specifications.
  - Emerging frameworks focus on interoperability, privacy preservation, and regulatory compliance.
