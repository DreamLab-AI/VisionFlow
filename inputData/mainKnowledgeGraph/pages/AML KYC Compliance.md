## AML KYC Compliance

AML KYC Compliance refers to a component of the blockchain ecosystem.

- Industry adoption and implementations
  - Financial institutions and Virtual Asset Service Providers (VASPs) globally have adopted robust AML KYC frameworks, integrating advanced technologies such as artificial intelligence and blockchain analytics.
  - Notable organisations and platforms include Chainalysis, Elliptic, TRM Labs, and Silent Eight, which provide transaction monitoring, wallet risk assessment, and identity verification services.
  - In the UK, major banks and fintech firms have implemented AI-driven AML solutions, with regional hubs in Manchester, Leeds, Newcastle, and Sheffield contributing to the development and deployment of compliance technologies.
  - For example, Manchester-based fintechs have partnered with local universities to pilot real-time transaction monitoring systems, while Leeds has seen growth in compliance-as-a-service offerings for small and medium enterprises.
- Technical capabilities and limitations
  - AI and machine learning algorithms have significantly improved the efficiency and accuracy of transaction monitoring, reducing false positives and enabling real-time detection of suspicious patterns.
  - Blockchain-based systems are increasingly used for identity verification and transaction monitoring, with approximately 15% of AML/KYC procedures conducted via blockchain-based systems by 2025.
  - Limitations include the complexity of integrating on-chain and off-chain data, the challenge of managing false positives, and the need for continuous updates to adapt to emerging threats and regulatory changes.
- Standards and frameworks
  - Key standards include the Financial Action Task Force (FATF) Recommendations, the EU’s Markets in Crypto-Assets (MiCA) regulation, and the UK’s Money Laundering Regulations 2017 (as amended).
  - Industry best practices emphasise the integration of Know Your Customer (KYC), Customer Due Diligence (CDD), Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD), and Know Your Transaction (KYT) processes.
  - Continuous screening against sanctions lists, known criminal addresses, and behavioural patterns is now considered standard practice.

  - The UK has been at the forefront of AML KYC compliance, with the Financial Conduct Authority (FCA) and HM Revenue & Customs (HMRC) playing key roles in shaping regulatory frameworks and enforcement.
