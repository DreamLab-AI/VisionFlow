## Decentralized Autonomous Organization

Decentralized Autonomous Organization refers to a component of the blockchain ecosystem.

- Industry adoption and implementations
  - DAOs are now widely used in decentralised finance (DeFi), NFT projects, and community-driven platforms
  - Notable organisations and platforms
    - Uniswap, Synthetix, and MakerDAO continue to be prominent examples of DAOs in the global ecosystem
    - In the UK, DAOs are increasingly adopted by fintech startups and digital collectives, with growing interest from traditional businesses exploring decentralised governance
  - UK and North England examples where relevant
    - Manchester-based digital collectives have experimented with DAOs for community funding and collaborative art projects
    - Leeds and Newcastle have seen the emergence of DAOs in local tech incubators, supporting open-source development and regional innovation
    - Sheffield’s digital economy hub has hosted workshops and hackathons focused on DAO governance and blockchain applications
- Technical capabilities and limitations
  - DAOs offer transparent, automated governance through smart contracts, reducing reliance on intermediaries and enabling global participation
  - Limitations include scalability challenges, regulatory uncertainty, and the potential for governance attacks or centralisation of voting power
- Standards and frameworks
  - Emerging standards for DAO governance, such as the DAOstack and Aragon frameworks, aim to improve interoperability and security
  - The UK’s Financial Conduct Authority (FCA) and other regulatory bodies are actively reviewing DAOs for compliance and legal recognition
